Cardiac Exertion Limits

The Logic & Formula

Max HR = 220 - Age
Ceiling = Max HR * (0.60 if HTN/MI else 0.70)
  • Baseline: established by Fox et al. (1971) as the universal physiological ceiling.
  • At-Risk Limit (60%): AHA guidelines dictate post-MI patients drastically increase ischemia risk beyond 60% exertion due to compromised ejection fraction.
  • Healthy Limit (70%): Caps youth (e.g., 19yo) at moderate aerobic limits to establish safe daily baseline habits.

Healthy Normal Range.

Clinical Justification

if (SpO2 < 94): trigger_danger_alert();

The World Health Organization (WHO) identifies sustained SpO2 readings of < 94% as clinical indicators of mild to moderate hypoxemia. Drops below this strictly enforced threshold lead directly to severe cognitive impairment, shortness of breath, and cellular damage. IntelliCure AI uses 94% as an immediate physical intervention trigger, regardless of age.

Sympathetic Arousal (Anxiety)

The "Fight-or-Flight" Biomarkers

HR > (5sec_Avg * 1.15)
AND
Temp < (5sec_Avg - 0.3°C)

When experiencing acute anxiety, the Sympathetic Nervous System activates. Tracking two contrasting metrics simultaneously eliminates false positives from physical exercise:

  • Chronotropy (HR Spike): Heart rate spontaneously surges without exertion.
  • Vasoconstriction (Temp Drop): Blood is shunted from extremities to the core, causing a rapid finger temperature drop ("cold sweat").
